The Digital Soul of a New Machine

An infographic visualizing the agent-based simulation of the Three-Bodied Economy. We stress-tested its foundational rules to see if a "consciousness-first" system could foster true collaboration or collapse into exploitation.

Section 1: The Emergent Social Fabric

Does the system's design naturally lead to collaborative "Guilds" or devolve into polarized "Cartels"? We modeled 10 distinct agent personas, each with unique motivations, to find out.

Persona Archetypes: A Clash of Motives

The simulation's outcome depends on the clash between pro-social agents, like "The Weaver" who seeks connection, and dissonant agents, like "The Accumulator" who prioritizes personal gain. This radar chart reveals their fundamentally different psychological profiles.

Section 2: The Social Immune System

We unleashed "red team" agents to find the system's breaking points. This analysis compares the effectiveness of different defenses against coordinated attacks on governance (SPK) and reputation (HEART).

Countermeasure Effectiveness

The data is clear: purely technical solutions are not enough. The most resilient system is a hybrid that makes collusion expensive (QV + Sybil Resistance) and culturally visible (the "Keeper" role).

Section 3: The Heart of Motivation

Can an economy run on purpose and belonging (HEART) instead of just money (SPK)? We simulated a market crash to test the resilience of economies with different incentive structures.

SPK-Dominant Economy

In an economy driven by financial rewards (extrinsic motivation), engagement is brittle. When the SPK token's value crashes, participation collapses as the primary incentive disappears.

HEART-Dominant Economy

In an economy driven by reputation and belonging (intrinsic motivation), engagement is resilient. Even after a market shock, agents continue to participate because their reward is the activity itself.
