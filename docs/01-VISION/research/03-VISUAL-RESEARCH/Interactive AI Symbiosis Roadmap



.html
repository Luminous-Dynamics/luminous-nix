Pillar 2: System Awareness

A true partner needs a sense of its environment. This section details how eBPF (Extended Berkeley Packet Filter) gives the AI "sensory perception" of the system's health and behavior. This provides a rich, real-time data stream, far superior to traditional monitoring, enabling a more accurate understanding of user state and system performance.

eBPF Toolchain Comparison

BCC is excellent for rapid prototyping. It bundles a compiler to build eBPF programs just-in-time, making it easy to experiment. However, its heavy dependencies (Clang/LLVM) make it less suitable for production.

libbpf with CO-RE (Compile Once - Run Everywhere) is the modern, production-ready choice. It uses pre-compiled, portable eBPF objects, resulting in lightweight, dependency-free applications ideal for deployment.

Pillar 3: Meta-Learning

Beyond simple learning, the AI must learn *how* to learn better. This is achieved through automated causal discovery, where the AI acts as a research scientist. It analyzes interaction data to hypothesize new cause-and-effect relationships, which are then validated by a human, allowing the AI to continuously improve its own "worldview."
